## Personal finance frameworks relevant to young adults

Budgeting frameworks are **decision shortcuts** (rules-of-thumb) that help people avoid ÔÇťblank-page budgeting.ÔÇŁ For 18ÔÇô30 year-olds with low-to-medium income, the best framework tends to be the one that:

### The four pillars approach

Because ÔÇťthe four pillarsÔÇŁ is used in **multiple** ways in personal finance and investing, itÔÇÖs useful to treat it as two complementary frameworks:

**Best suited for:**

#### Four pillars of investing (Bernstein)

## Common personal finance mistakes among young adults in Italy and Europe

The most frequent mistakes cluster into:  
- **behavioral drift** (impulse spending, subscriptions, social-driven purchases),  
- **missing buffers** (weak emergency savings),  
- **short-term credit traps** (BNPL, revolving credit, installment misunderstandings), and  
- **concept gaps** (interest, compound interest, inflation, diversification).

### What recent Italian evidence says about ages 18ÔÇô34

The **Bank of Italy** ran a dedicated survey on young adults **18ÔÇô34** in Italy (fielded in **Q1 2023**, nearly **5,400** respondents). ţłÇciteţłéturn3view0ţłéturn2search7ţłü Key findings directly map to ÔÇťmistakesÔÇŁ:
