# How to build a budget

**The starting point of every financial plan**

---

## What comes in, what goes out

A budget tracks everything you **earn** and everything you **spend**.

- **Income** → take-home pay, freelance, passive income
- **Fixed expenses** → rent, utilities, subscriptions, loan payments
- **Variable expenses** → groceries, dining, entertainment, clothing

---

## Your spending margin

$$\text{Margin} = \text{Income} - \text{Total expenses}$$

**Positive** → you have room to save or invest.

**Negative** → you're consuming savings. Address this first.

---

## The 50 / 30 / 20 rule

| Category | % of take-home | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Needs | 50% | Rent, food, transport |
| Wants | 30% | Restaurants, travel, streaming |
| Savings | 20% | Emergency fund, investments |

> A starting point, not a law. Adjust to your situation.

---

## Your first goal: the emergency fund

Before investing, build **3 months of expenses** in a liquid account.

This protects your plan from unexpected events without taking on debt.

**Example:** spending €1,200/month → target: €3,600 saved.
